Week 5 · Unit Framing Question

"How does viewing climate change as a complex adaptive system reshape the way we learn, teach, and respond to it?"

In Part 1 we examined our entanglement with Earth, our biases and worldviews, and the social justice dimensions of the climate crisis. Part 2 zooms out — to planetary systems, interdisciplinary frameworks, and the relational, emergent nature of the challenges we face. This is the lens that will carry us through the next five weeks.

Module Overview

From cause-and-effect to systems thinking

Linear thinking — "CO₂ rises → temperature increases → bad things happen" — is true but dangerously incomplete. This module introduces the framework that better matches the actual complexity of what we're facing.

This week pairs the module with three required readings — they're linked right where you need them, so you're not juggling tabs. Going through the module first means the readings will click faster, since you'll already recognize the ideas. All in, plan for about 50 minutes, but there's no rush — work through it at whatever pace fits your day.
